The software world moves fast—and with AI, it’s accelerating even more.

What I share here may become outdated in just a few months, or lose its shine compared to the latest breakthroughs. Still, I believe that at any point in time, the **experience**, **approach**, and **perspective** gained from building real systems can offer some value—however small—to others. If something on this site helps you understand a topic more clearly, work a little better, or simply think about a problem differently, that’s already enough.

In my work, I’ve had the chance to explore and apply a variety of methods to **build** and **ship** software systems in production. My role isn’t limited to implementation—I also try to **collect**, **distill**, and **share** practical ways of working with a wider software engineering community.

### what I write about

- **systems** — Building real-world systems: design choices, trade-offs, operations, and post-launch lessons.
- **engineering** — Software engineering: process, code quality, reviews, testing, CI/CD, and observability.
- **AI** — AI in products: practical ways to apply AI/LLMs, balancing effectiveness, cost, reliability, and risk.
- **notes** — Short notes: small but useful building blocks from day-to-day work.

### sharing principles

- **practical** — Focus on what can be applied, measured, and validated—not just what sounds good.
- **context** — No universal best solution—everything depends on the problem and constraints.
- **concise** — Respect your time: keep it short when possible, go deep when necessary.
